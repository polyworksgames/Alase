Re-Entry Services

Bridging the gap from incarceration to independence — comprehensive, trauma-informed support for returning citizens every step of the way

How We Help Returning Citizens

A comprehensive continuum of support from release to stability

Returning to the community after incarceration is one of life's most challenging transitions. The first days, weeks, and months after release are critical — and the barriers are significant: lack of housing, employment history gaps, limited transportation, strained family relationships, and unresolved mental health or substance use issues can quickly derail even the most motivated individual.

At Alase Center for Enrichment, we provide a comprehensive, trauma-informed continuum of re-entry services designed to address these barriers head-on. Our experienced team of peer support specialists — many of whom have lived experience with incarceration — walks alongside returning citizens every step of the way.

Transitional Housing Support

We connect individuals with safe, stable transitional housing options and provide advocacy to overcome the barriers many returning citizens face in securing housing. We work with landlords, housing programs, and government resources to find suitable placements.

Employment Readiness

Our employment coaches help participants build job-ready skills — from resume writing and interview preparation to identifying "ban-the-box" friendly employers and navigating background check disclosures. We help individuals present their strengths confidently.

Documentation Assistance

We assist with obtaining critical identification documents — state ID, Social Security cards, birth certificates — that are essential for accessing housing, employment, benefits, and other services.

Mental Health & Substance Use Support

Returning citizens often carry unresolved trauma, mental health conditions, and substance use challenges. We connect individuals with licensed mental health professionals and peer recovery coaches who provide culturally competent, trauma-informed care.

Education & Skills Training

We help individuals explore educational opportunities — GED completion, vocational training, community college enrollment — and connect them with scholarship resources available to those with criminal records.

Family Reunification & Support

We provide counseling and mediation services to help rebuild healthy family relationships. Strong family connections are one of the most powerful protective factors against recidivism.

Legal Navigation Support

We help individuals understand their legal obligations during supervision (parole/probation), navigate compliance requirements, and connect with legal aid organizations for expungements, restoration of rights, and other legal needs.

Peer Mentoring

Our certified peer support specialists — individuals who have themselves navigated the criminal justice system and successfully rebuilt their lives — provide authentic, empathetic mentoring that no textbook can replicate. They are proof that change is possible.
