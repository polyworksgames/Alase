Our Approach to Sex Offender Treatment

Evidence-based, clinically rigorous, and community-focused

Sex offenses represent one of the most complex and stigmatized areas of criminal justice. At Alase Center for Enrichment, we provide specialized, clinically rigorous treatment and support services for individuals who have been convicted of sexual offenses — with community safety, victim awareness, and genuine accountability at the center of everything we do.

Our programs are grounded in the Good Lives Model (GLM) and Risk-Need-Responsivity (RNR) framework — the current gold standards in sex offender treatment. These evidence-based approaches focus not only on reducing risk, but on helping individuals build fulfilling, pro-social lives that reduce the motivation to reoffend.

Our services are designed for individuals who are court-mandated to treatment, voluntarily seeking help, or transitioning from institutional settings into community supervision. All services comply with North Carolina statutes, SORA (Sex Offender Registration and Notification Act) requirements, and professional ethical guidelines.

What We Provide

Individual Therapy

One-on-one clinical sessions with Dr. Anthony Smith, PhD and our licensed clinicians address offense-related cognitions, deviant arousal patterns, emotional regulation, empathy development, and relapse prevention planning using evidence-based modalities including Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT).

Group Therapy

Structured group treatment provides peer accountability, social skills development, and a space for processing offense cycles, victim impact, and healthy relationship-building. Group settings have been shown to be particularly effective in sex offender treatment.

Relapse Prevention Planning

Participants develop personalized safety plans that identify offense-specific risk factors, early warning signs, and concrete strategies to interrupt the offense cycle before harm occurs. Plans are regularly reviewed and updated with the treatment team.

Victim Empathy & Impact Education

A cornerstone of our program is developing genuine understanding of the harm caused by sexual offenses. Through structured exercises, readings, and guided reflection, participants confront the impact of their actions and develop authentic empathy for those they have harmed.

Housing & Community Placement

We help registered sex offenders navigate the complex restrictions on housing while finding stable, compliant living arrangements. We work collaboratively with probation/parole officers, local government, and housing providers to identify suitable options.

Registration Compliance Support

Understanding and complying with Sex Offender Registration requirements is critical. We help individuals understand their obligations, maintain compliance, and avoid violations that could result in additional charges or incarceration.

Family & Support System Education

Families and support persons play a vital role in successful reintegration. We offer education and counseling to help loved ones understand sex offending behavior, set appropriate boundaries, and be a positive force in the reintegration process.

Internet Safety & Technology Management

For many sex offenders, particularly those convicted of internet-based offenses, technology management is a key component of treatment. We provide education and monitoring support to ensure appropriate and compliant technology use.

Our Clinical Approach

01

Risk Assessment

Using validated actuarial tools (Static-99R, STABLE-2007, ACUTE-2007), we conduct thorough risk assessments to develop individualized treatment plans that address each person's specific risk factors and needs.

02

Individualized Treatment Planning

No two individuals are the same. Treatment plans are tailored to each person's risk level, needs, responsivity factors, and personal strengths — focusing on building a meaningful, pro-social life.

03

Ongoing Collaboration

We maintain close collaboration with probation/parole officers, courts, and other treatment providers. Regular progress reports and case conferencing ensure a coordinated, effective community response.

04

Community Safety First

Community protection is our primary obligation. Our treatment model is designed to reduce recidivism and create safer communities by addressing the root causes of sexual offending.

Who We Serve

Court-Mandated Individuals

Those ordered by the courts or probation/parole to complete sex offender treatment as a condition of their supervision.

Voluntary Participants

Individuals who are proactively seeking treatment to address problematic behaviors and reduce their risk before harm occurs.

Recently Released Individuals

Those transitioning from prisons, detention centers, or residential treatment facilities back into community supervision.

Registered Sex Offenders

Individuals on the sex offender registry who need ongoing treatment, compliance support, and community reintegration assistance.
